# Roofing Image Detail QA

Use this when generating or reviewing roofing image ads, especially before/after proof creatives.

## Why it matters
Roofing proof sells through detail. If shingles look mushy, smeared, or textureless, the image stops reading as real project proof and starts reading as AI filler.

## Shingle detail requirements
A usable roofing image should show:
- visible individual shingle rows
- dimensional asphalt texture / granule variation
- believable roof pitch and plane geometry
- clean flashing around chimney/dormers/valleys
- straight gutters, fascia, siding, and roof edges
- no melted, blurred, tiled, or overly smooth roof surface

## Before/after rules
- Prefer real before and after project references when available.
- Use image-to-image with both references for same-house transformations.
- Label the prompt clearly: reference 1 = BEFORE, reference 2 = AFTER.
- Preserve house identity: dormer, chimney, siding, trim, roof pitch, windows, and camera angle.
- The “before” can be stained/aged but should not be cartoonishly destroyed.
- The “after” should be clean and detailed, not plastic-smooth.

## Prompt language that helped
Use wording like:

```text
Preserve high shingle detail, clear individual shingle rows, realistic granule texture, roof pitch, dormer, chimney, beige siding, white trim, gutters, and Maryland suburban context. Do not blur or smooth the shingles. No warped rooflines, no melted shingle texture, no duplicate chimneys.
```

## QA checklist before posting
- Zoom/vision-check the roof surface, not just the overall composition.
- If the shingles lack detail, regenerate before Slack review.
- If a weak roofing asset was posted, delete it and replace it rather than letting it stay in the approval set.
