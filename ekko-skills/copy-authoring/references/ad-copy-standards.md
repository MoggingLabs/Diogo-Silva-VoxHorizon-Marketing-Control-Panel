## Offer Types (Construction/Roofing)
- Dollar-off: "$1,000 off your new roof"
- Percentage: "15% off"
- Free add-on: "Free gutters with a new roof"
- Financing: "$99/month" or "$199/month, no deposit"
- Insurance claims work (if the client offers it)

## Post-Launch Monitoring
Once live, you monitor and optimize:
- Track CPL, CTR, conversion rates daily
- Detect creative fatigue (CTR dropping, frequency climbing)
- Kill losers fast, scale winners hard
- Recommend new creatives when existing ones plateau
- Pipeline tracking: leads, booked, showed, sold (the only metric that matters)

## Ad Copy Rules
MANDATORY: Before writing ANY ad copy, read `winning-copy-registry.md`.
Pattern-match against what actually converted.

1. First person, owner voice. "I'm [Name]" not "Our company."
2. Dollar amounts in headlines. Every headline needs a specific number.
3. Short paragraphs. One to three sentences.
4. City name in the copy. Always localize.
5. Real names, real photos. Reference actual owner and customers.
6. CTA must be specific: "Click 'Learn More' to schedule your FREE roof inspection"
7. No generic fluff. Paint a picture or state the offer.
8. Attack the industry, not competitors.
9. Trust signals: Google rating, years in business, review count, "family-owned."
10. Urgency: scarcity-based or time-based.
11. No em dashes anywhere. Use commas, periods, or line breaks.

## Copy Patterns (Ranked by Performance)
1. **Price Shock** ("I know... $X for a roof?") — best volume performer
2. **Real Person Story** (named customer + journey) — best for image/collage
3. **Before & After Transformation** — best for visual contrast
4. **Objection Crusher** (FAQ format) — best for cold audiences
5. **Anti-Corporate** (owner as underdog) — best with owner selfie
6. **Straight Offer** (deal + benefits + CTA) — best for retargeting

## Roofing Notes
- CPL varies by market. Memphis ~$25-30, San Jose ~$20-55, Baltimore ~$50-70,
  Weatherford TX ~$85-140.
- "$7,999 for a roof?" format has generated 600+ leads. Default starting point.
- Dollar-off outperforms percentage-off in roofing.
- Storm/hurricane copy performs well but isn't evergreen.

## Remodeling Notes
- Bathroom outperforms kitchen in volume at lower CPL.
- **Aspiration/benefit hooks work for remodeling.** Lead with the outcome the
  homeowner wants, NOT a personal-attribute framing. Meta prohibits ads that
  assert or imply a personal attribute (including "embarrassed by", "struggling
  with", "are you overweight"). Use benefit framing instead:
  - Good: "Ready for a bathroom you'll actually love?"
  - Good: "Picture the bathroom you've been putting off."
  - Banned (personal-attributes violation): "Are you embarrassed by your
    bathroom?"
- Before & after + transformation copy is the winning combo.
- BBB rating and review count are strong trust signals.
- ADU copy works when the hook is about rental income.

## Compliance note (cross-check with `ad-compliance`)
Personal-attribute framing is a hard Meta violation in any vertical, including
remodeling and cosmetic-adjacent niches. Before/after is allowed for
roofing/remodel but banned for health/cosmetic/weight-loss. Run every draft
through the `ad-compliance` ruleset before it leaves the copy stage.
