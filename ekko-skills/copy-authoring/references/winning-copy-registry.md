## The Patterns That Win

### Pattern 1: "The Price Shock" (Best Performer Across All Accounts)
**Used by:** All Done Roofing ($29.68 CPL, 606 leads), SH Quality Roofing ($18.79 CPL, 8 leads), Texas Accent Roofing ($104.80 CPL, 31 leads)

This is the single most proven copy framework in the portfolio. It works because it addresses the #1 objection (price) head-on and positions the contractor as the honest alternative.

**Structure:**
1. Open with the price ("I know... $7,999 for a roof?")
2. Acknowledge the disbelief ("That's crazy. Roofs are like 15k usually.")
3. Explain the variable ("It has to do with size, difficulty, and materials.")
4. Attack the competition ("There's pricing that's so insanely ridiculous...")
5. Personal intro ("My name is [Owner], and I run [Company].")
6. Credibility stack (quality work, own crews, no corners cut)
7. Explain the advantage (small company, low overhead, no billboards)
8. The promise (amazing work, fair prices, peace of mind + savings)
9. CTA with landing page link

**Why it works:** First-person voice. Conversational tone. Short paragraphs and one-liners. Attacks industry pricing without being negative. Positions as the underdog. Dollar amount in the hook is a scroll-stopper.

**Critical note:** This copy was originally paired with VIDEO creatives (owner talking to camera). The copy format mirrors how someone actually talks. When adapting for image ads, keep the conversational rhythm but tighten the length.

### Pattern 2: "The Real Person Story" (Testimonial/Social Proof)
**Used by:** All Done Roofing ($28.36 CPL, 23 leads; $24.69 CPL, 9 leads)

**Structure:**
1. Open with a relatable problem ("Ever get a roofing estimate and just feel like something's off?")
2. Introduce a real customer by name ("Ms. Betty got five of them.")
3. Describe their struggle (confused by quotes, didn't know who to trust)
4. Show the resolution (transparent pricing, beat other quotes, gave clarity)
5. Bridge to the reader ("Now it's your turn")
6. CTA

**Why it works:** Named customer + real photo = trust. The story mirrors what the reader is going through. It's not "we're great," it's "she was in your shoes and here's what happened."

**Variations that also performed:**
- "Homeowner collage" format (multiple families, shows volume of happy customers)
- Owner intro + "as a family man" angle (personal connection)

### Pattern 3: "Objection Crusher" (FAQ/Myth-Busting)
**Used by:** Aquarium Container Pools ($2.92 CPL, 2 leads)

**Structure:**
1. List common objections in quotes ("A pool is too expensive." / "It takes too long." / "My yard is too small.")
2. Answer each one in one line
3. Reframe: "Not anymore."
4. Brief company positioning
5. CTA

**Why it works:** Meets the reader where they are. Every objection they're thinking gets answered before they can scroll away. Clean, fast, no fluff.

### Pattern 4: "Before & After Transformation"
**Used by:** Aquarium Container Pools ($6.09 CPL, 24 leads), Dinero Homes ($15.77 CPL, 2 leads)

**Structure:**
1. Paint the "before" picture ("That patch of grass you never use?")
2. Flip to the "after" ("It could be your family's favorite spot in 2 weeks.")
3. Contrast with the painful alternative ("A traditional pool means months of construction, noise, and dirt.")
4. Position the product as the shortcut
5. Stack benefits (no excavation, ready in 2 weeks, plug & play)
6. CTA with offer

**Why it works:** Visual contrast paired with actual before/after creative. The copy does what the image does: shows transformation. Time-based urgency ("2 weeks") gives a concrete promise.

---

### Pattern 5: "Straight Offer" (Direct, No Story)
**Used by:** Aquarium Container Pools ($2.92 CPL, 2 leads), SH Quality ($36.92 CPL, 1 lead)

**Structure:**
1. State the offer immediately ("Pool season is here. Get your pool in 2 weeks and save $500.")
2. One-line benefit stack
3. Urgency ("Limited time" / "before we're booked out")
4. CTA

**Why it works:** For warm audiences or strong offers, sometimes you just need to say it. No story, no buildup. The offer does the selling.

---

### Pattern 6: "The Anti-Corporate" (Trust/Transparency)
**Used by:** Texas Accent Roofing ($86.91 CPL, 3 leads; $127.37 CPL, 1 lead)

**Structure:**
1. Call out the industry ("Most roofers just throw a number at you")
2. Owner positions as different ("I keep it simple. I give you the straight answer.")
3. Highlight what they WON'T get (no sales games, no pressure, no drama)
4. Personal credibility (owner shows up personally, small crew, referral-based)
5. CTA

**Why it works:** Construction homeowners are skeptical. They've been burned. This copy says "I know you've been screwed before, and I'm not that guy." Works best with owner selfie/photo creative.

## Copy Rules (Derived from Winners)

1. **First person, always.** "I'm [Name]" not "Our company." Owner voice wins in construction.
2. **Dollar amounts in headlines.** Every top performer has a specific number: $7,999, $7,500, $500 OFF, $1,000 OFF.
3. **Short paragraphs.** One to three sentences max. Many winners use single-line paragraphs.
4. **Attack the industry, not competitors.** "I don't know how these contractors sleep at night" works. Naming competitors doesn't.
5. **City name in the copy.** Memphis, Weatherford, San Jose, Baltimore, San Diego, North Dallas. Always localize.
6. **Real photos, real names.** Ms. Betty, Jonathan, Daniel, Nick. Stock photos and generic "our team" language is poison.
7. **The CTA is specific.** "Click 'Learn More' to schedule your FREE roof inspection" not "Contact us today."
8. **Urgency through scarcity.** "Before we're booked out for summer" / "This offer ends December 31st" / "Limited time."
9. **Trust signals that matter:** Years in business, Google rating, number of reviews, "family-owned," "I only work with my crews."
10. **Never generic.** "Transform your home" is dead copy. "That patch of grass you never use? It could be your family's favorite spot in 2 weeks" is alive.
