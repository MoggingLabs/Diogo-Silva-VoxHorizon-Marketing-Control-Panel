# Mignogna construction copy patterns

Use when writing construction/roofing/remodeling ads for Diogo/VoxHorizon, especially when CPL is bleeding and speed matters.

Source context: Diogo provided a winning ad copy vault from Michael Mignogna, a construction niche agency operator with 10+ years of experience. Diogo explicitly said this is the kind of copy he likes because it feels personal to the homeowner.

## Core read

The copy should sound like a contractor leveling with a homeowner, not a brand running a polished campaign. The power comes from plain speech, homeowner skepticism, and explaining the roofing pricing game.

## Patterns to borrow

- Fair price triangle: cheapest guy cuts corners, expensive guy passes overhead to the homeowner, we live in the fair middle.
- Dirty little secret: prices are often inflated to cover bloated company overhead.
- Owner/family/local trust: first-person or close company voice, real people, years in business, reviews, local roots.
- Price skepticism hook: `$7,500 for a roof?` then explain size, difficulty, materials, and fair pricing.
- Financing relief: roof is expensive, but payments can make action feel manageable.
- Insurance/storm urgency only when true: damage windows close, waiting turns one problem into three.
- No-pressure quote: free inspection, straight answer, second opinion, no kitchen-table pressure show.

## Rhythm

- Short broken-line paragraphs.
- Rhetorical questions.
- Simple words.
- Specific homeowner objections.
- Avoid polished brand language like `transform your home`, `premium solutions`, `trusted experts` unless grounded by real proof.

## Launch-copy shape

For each creative, produce three variants when asked:

1. Long: full objection handling and story.
2. Medium: same angle tightened for launch speed.
3. Medium-short: sharp hook, proof, CTA.

Headlines should be interchangeable within the same creative group and include concrete offers/proof where possible: price/monthly payment, free inspection, trusted local roofers, years in business.

Do not let headlines collapse into generic offer labels like `Free Roof Inspection`, `Roof Financing Available`, or `Baltimore Roof Replacement` as the whole set. At least one lead headline per creative should carry a homeowner objection or felt problem:
- `Need a roof without quote games?`
- `A new roof without the lump sum?`
- `Repair or replace? Know first.`
- `Got a quote? Check it first.`

Use plain sentence case when it reads better on mobile. Offer-label headlines can stay as secondary variants, but they should not be the only position.

Do not let all three variants for one ad start with the same first line. Keep the angle consistent, but vary the opener so Ads Manager previews do not look duplicated. Example for quote-check copy: long `Already got a roofing quote?`, medium `Have a roofing quote in hand?`, medium-short `Staring at a roofing quote right now?`.

## Quality bar

If it could be used by any roofing company in any city, it is too generic. Add local proof, real people, years/reviews, a homeowner objection, or a specific pricing/payment claim.
