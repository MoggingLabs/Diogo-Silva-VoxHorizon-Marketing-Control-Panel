# Roofing ad copy patterns

Use this reference when writing paid ad copy for roofing contractors, especially rush-launch packages where CPL is bleeding and speed matters.

## Winning voice
- First person owner/family voice beats corporate voice.
- Make it feel like the homeowner is being leveled with, not sold to.
- Talk directly to the confusion homeowners feel around roofing prices.
- Short paragraphs. One idea per line when the rhythm benefits from it.
- Plain words: cheap, stupid expensive, fair price, straight answer, pressure games.

## Core winning angles

### Cheapest vs most expensive
Structure:
1. "Are we the cheapest? No."
2. Explain cheap roof risk: cut corners, junk materials, surprise costs, disappears later.
3. "Are we the most expensive? Not even close."
4. Explain bloated-company overhead: offices, sales teams, trucks, billboards, TV.
5. Position as the fair middle: quality work, good materials, sane pricing.
6. CTA for free quote/inspection.

### Dirty little secret / overhead
Structure:
1. "Dirty little secret about roofing:"
2. Homeowners often pay for overhead, not a better roof.
3. Name the overhead: fancy offices, sales teams, ads everywhere.
4. Contrast with the client: lean/local/family owned.
5. CTA for a free inspection or quote.

### Price shock / starting price
Structure:
1. Open with the number: "$X for a roof?"
2. Acknowledge disbelief: "Roofs are like 15k usually, right?"
3. Explain that size, difficulty, and materials change the price.
4. Say there is a fair price and an insane price.
5. Owner intro and credibility.
6. Explain low overhead/quality materials/fair margins.
7. CTA.

### Trust/community creative
Use when the image is a real owner/family/community/proof photo rather than a roof transformation.
Structure:
1. Name what is in the photo.
2. Explain why it matters to the homeowner.
3. Tie it to trust: shows up, explains the work, uses the right materials, stays reachable.
4. Local proof: years in business, reviews, family owned, city/service area.
5. CTA.

## Rush launch package format
When the user asks to launch multiple ad creatives quickly:
- Produce copy in channel-ready format.
- For each creative: 1 long, 1 medium, 1 medium-short primary text.
- Provide 3 headlines per creative that can be mixed with any of the copy variants.
- Add a short launch note naming the strongest pairing and why.
- Avoid long rationale before the deliverable. Speed matters when CPL is bleeding.

## Guardrails
- Do not over-polish into generic "transform/protect/quality service" sludge.
- Do not make hard price claims unless provided by the user/client context.
- If using insurance/deductible framing, avoid promising approval or implying homeowners can avoid required deductibles.
- Keep public-facing output clean: no task IDs, internal analysis, or tool chatter.
